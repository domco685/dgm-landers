High Income. Low Fulfillment. That Was Me.

How I lost 100 lbs, quit drinking, and saved $1M—by changing who I believed I was

I was sitting in a conference room at Facebook in 2019, staring at my laptop screen.

Salary: $240,000 a year.

Stock options worth another $100K.

Title that made my mom proud.

And I felt... nothing.

Actually, that's not true. I felt empty. Hollow. Like I was watching someone else live my life.

The Achievement Trap

For 15 years, I did everything right.

Got the degree. Landed the job. Climbed the ladder. Made the money.

Facebook. Then Shopify. Director-level by 35. The whole package.

But here's what nobody tells you about "making it": The emptiness doesn't go away when you hit six figures. It gets worse.

Because now you don't even have an excuse. You're supposed to be happy. You have everything you worked for.

So why do you feel like this?

The Morning Routine That Wasn't

My alarm went off at 6:47 AM.

Not 6:45. Not 7:00. 6:47.

I'd read some article about how successful people wake up early, so I set this random time to feel like I was optimizing something.

I'd hit snooze twice. Scroll Instagram for 20 minutes. Feel guilty about both.

At 275 pounds, I couldn't see my feet in the shower. My wedding ring cut into my finger. I'd stopped looking at photos of myself.

But the worst part wasn't the weight.

It was the drinking.

Two Bottles of Wine. Every Night.

I didn't think I had a problem.

I was a "functional drinker." I never missed work. Never got a DUI. Never had a dramatic breakdown.

I just... needed it.

So I'd pour a glass of wine at 6 PM. Then another at 7. By 9, I'd finished the bottle. By 10, I'd opened a second.

My wife stopped commenting on it after a while. What was she going to say? I wasn't falling down drunk. I was just... numbing out.

Every single night. For five years.

The $1 Million I Didn't Have

Here's the math that broke me:

$240K salary at Facebook. Three years. $180K at Shopify before that. Four years. $120K at my first startup. Two years.

Total earned over 9 years: Roughly $1.6 million before taxes.

Amount in my bank account in 2019: $87,000.

Where did $1 million go? I don't know.

I made more money than 94% of Americans. And I had nothing to show for it except a gut that hung over my belt and a nightly wine habit.

The Moment Everything Changed

October 12, 2019. I remember the date because it was my daughter's 6th birthday party.

We were at the park. She was running around with her friends. I was sitting on a bench, sweating through my shirt, out of breath from walking 100 yards from the car.

Another dad—a guy I barely knew—sat down next to me.

"You doing okay, man?"

I said I was fine. Just tired from work.

He nodded. Didn't push it.

Then he said something I'll never forget:

"I used to feel like that too. Like I was living someone else's life. Turns out I was."

The Identity I Didn't Choose

That night, I couldn't stop thinking about what he said. Living someone else's life.

Because that's exactly what it felt like.

I wasn't the guy who drank two bottles of wine every night. That wasn't me. I wasn't the guy who weighed 275 pounds. That wasn't me either.

But... it was. Because that's what I did. Every. Single. Day.

And here's what I finally understood:

You don't do what you think. You do what you believe you are.

I believed I was:

  • A stressed executive who needed wine to relax
  • A busy professional who didn't have time to work out
  • A successful person who deserved to spend money on whatever I wanted

Those beliefs ran my life. Not my goals. Not my plans. Not my "intentions." My identity.

The 30-Day Experiment

I didn't try to fix everything at once.

I didn't set a goal to lose 100 pounds. I didn't vow to never drink again. I didn't create a budget or hire a financial planner.

Instead, I did something different.

I asked myself: Who is the version of me that doesn't need wine to relax?

And I started acting like that person.

Not by forcing habits. Not by willpower. Not by some 47-step morning routine.

By deciding who I was... and then noticing when I acted out of alignment with that identity.

I called it an identity shift. And I gave myself 30 days to see if it worked.

90 Days Later

Three months after that birthday party:

  • Weight: 242 pounds (down 33)
  • Alcohol: Zero drinks
  • Savings: $14,000 in the bank
  • Energy: I could run up stairs without stopping

But here's what really changed:

I woke up at 6:30 AM without an alarm. I looked in the mirror and recognized myself. I showed up at work clear-headed, confident, present.

My wife said something that made me cry: "It feels like I got my husband back."

Two Years Later

Today, I weigh 190 pounds. I haven't had a drink in 847 days. I've saved over $180,000.

But the number that matters most: I feel like myself again.

Not the self I was pretending to be for 15 years. The self I always was, buried under achievement, pressure, and numbing.
