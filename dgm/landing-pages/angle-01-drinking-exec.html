The 3-Minute Bedtime Habit That Helped a Facebook Exec Quit Drinking—Without Willpower

The Breaking Point

I was standing in my kitchen at 2 AM, holding an empty bottle of whiskey, staring at my reflection in the microwave door.

275 pounds. Puffy face. Dead eyes.

I was a growth lead at Facebook. Then Shopify. I'd scaled products to millions of users. I knew how to optimize funnels, crush KPIs, and lead teams.

But I couldn't stop drinking.

Twenty years I'd been trying. Twenty years of "this time will be different."

White-knuckling through day 3, then collapsing on day 4. Discipline. Willpower. Accountability partners. Apps. Therapists. Books.

Nothing worked.

Because I was fighting the wrong battle.

The Discovery That Changed Everything

In 2019, I did something desperate.

I flew to Costa Rica for an ayahuasca ceremony.

I'm not woo-woo. I'm a data guy. But I was out of options. I thought maybe I'd find some mystical cure, some spiritual reset button.

What I got instead was brutal clarity.

During the ceremony, I heard a voice—my voice—crystal clear:

"You drink because you can't control your feelings."

Not because I lacked discipline. Not because I was weak. Not because I hadn't read the right book or found the right system.

I drank because I was terrified of feeling.

Success felt like pressure. Failure felt like death. Emptiness felt like falling. So I numbed it all.

The ayahuasca didn't fix me. But it showed me the real problem.

And once I knew that, everything changed.

The Book That Unlocked the Solution

A week after I got back from Costa Rica, someone recommended a book: Feeling is the Secret by Neville Goddard.

70 pages. Written in the 1940s. Looked like self-help nonsense.

But the premise hit different:

Your subconscious mind speaks the language of feelings.

Not thoughts. Not affirmations. Not vision boards.

Feelings.

If you want to change your behavior, you don't need more willpower. You need to change the feeling that's driving the behavior.

I was drinking to escape bad feelings. But what if I could generate the feelings I actually wanted?

What if I could teach my nervous system that I was already the person who didn't need alcohol?

That's when I built the system.

The ASKING Framework: 3 Minutes to Rewire Your Identity

I'm an engineer at heart. I needed a process.

So I reverse-engineered Neville Goddard's work and built the ASKING framework:

  • Awareness → Notice what you're feeling right now
  • Sensations → Describe it in your body (where, how intense, texture)
  • Knowing → Ask: "What do I know to be true about myself right now?"
  • Imagine → Who is the version of me that doesn't have this problem?
  • Nurture → Feel what that person feels. Embody it. Breathe it in.
  • Generate → Carry that feeling into your day

It takes 3 minutes.

I did it every night before bed. Then again in the morning. Then a quick reflection at night.

I didn't "quit drinking." I became someone who didn't drink.

What Happened Next

Within 30 days, I stopped craving alcohol.

Not through discipline. Through identity shift.

My nervous system started to believe: "I'm the guy who doesn't drink."

And once that became my default feeling? The behavior followed effortlessly.

  • Launched my own business (no longer numbing my ambition)

All from the same 3-minute practice.

Because once you learn to control your feelings, you control everything.
